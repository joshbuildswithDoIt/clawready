Lobster Condo vs Cloud Hosting: Why Physical Hardware Matters

Everyone tells you to put your AI agent on a $5 VPS. For many people, that's fine. But there's a growing group who've tried cloud — and want something different.

12 min read · March 2026 · By the ClawReady team

The Cloud Hosting Reality

Let's be fair about what cloud hosting does well:

  • Instant setup (minutes, not days)
  • No upfront hardware cost
  • Easy to scale up or down
  • Someone else handles infrastructure
  • Available from anywhere in the world

For a basic agent that sends calendar reminders and answers quick questions, a $10-20/month VPS is perfect. We're not going to pretend otherwise.

But as people run more serious agents — 24/7 autonomous workflows, local model inference, multi-agent systems — the cracks start showing.

Five Problems Cloud Hosting Can't Solve

1. The Noisy Neighbor Problem

On shared cloud infrastructure, your agent competes with other customers for CPU, memory, and I/O. VPS providers oversell capacity. That "4GB RAM, 2 vCPU" instance shares physical hardware with dozens of other VMs.

✅ With dedicated hardware: Your Mac Mini's 16GB of RAM is YOUR 16GB. Nobody else touches it. Consistent performance, 24/7.

2. No Local Model Inference

Running local AI models requires real hardware. Cloud options: standard VPS can't run models, GPU instances cost $360-2,160/month.

A $400 used Mac Mini with 16GB RAM runs Qwen 2.5 7B at 30+ tokens/second. A $20/month VPS can't even load the model.

✅ The math: If you spend $30/month on API calls a local model could handle, a Mac Mini pays for itself in 13 months. After that — free inference forever.

3. You Don't Own Anything

Cloud hosting is renting. When you stop paying: your agent disappears, your data is deleted, your configurations, memories, and trained behaviors — gone.

✅ With physical hardware: You own the machine. If you ever leave Lobster Condo, you take your Mac Mini with you. Your agent, your data, your hardware.

4. The Trust Gap

Your AI agent has access to your API keys (worth money), messages (sensitive), memory files (personal), and business workflows (competitive). On shared cloud, the hosting provider's employees could theoretically access your VM.

✅ With dedicated hardware in a facility you can visit: Your agent runs on a machine you can physically see and touch. Nobody else has access.

5. No Community, No Ownership

Cloud hosting is transactional. You're a customer number. Lobster Condo is building something different:

  • Founding member council — your voice shapes how the facility runs
  • Transparent books — monthly reports on where every dollar goes
  • Shared upside — loyalty perks, rate locks, referral bonuses
  • Physical community — visit, meet other operators, climb some walls

🤝 Who Should Stay on Cloud

We're being honest — cloud hosting is better if you:

  • Run a lightweight agent (simple assistant, no local models)
  • Need to scale quickly or unpredictably
  • Are far from Cleveland (latency matters for some use cases)
  • Spend less than $20/month total
  • Want zero hardware commitment

Managed cloud options we respect: RunMyClaw ($30/mo), xCloud ($24/mo), OpenClaw Cloud ($40/mo).
